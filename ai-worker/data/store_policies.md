# Smart Warehouse — Store Policy Guidelines
# Effective Date: January 1, 2025
# Document Version: 3.2

---

## 1. Near-Expiry Discount Authorization Policy

### 1.1 Dairy & Fresh Consumables
Products classified under **Dairy**, **Fresh Produce**, **Bakery**, and **Deli** categories
follow an accelerated markdown schedule due to their short shelf life:

- **Within 5 days of expiry date**: Store managers are authorized to apply a
  **"Buy 1 Get 1 Free" (BOGO)** promotion or a **50% discount** on the retail price.
  Both promotional formats may be applied without district manager approval.
- **Within 3 days of expiry date**: Products should be moved to the designated
  "Quick Sale" refrigerated endcap and clearly labeled with bright-yellow markdown tags.
- **On expiry date or past expiry**: Products must be **immediately pulled from shelves**,
  logged in the waste management system, and disposed of per local health code regulations.
  No sales are permitted on or after the expiry date for any fresh consumable.

### 1.2 Over-the-Counter (OTC) Medications
Products classified under **OTC Medications**, **Health Supplements**, and **First Aid**
follow a longer clearance window:

- **Within 30 days of expiry date**: Authorize a **30% markdown** from the retail price.
  Products must be relocated to the dedicated **"Clearance Shelf"** near the pharmacy counter.
  Shelf signage must clearly state: *"Clearance — Approaching Best-By Date"*.
- **Within 14 days of expiry date**: Increase the markdown to **50% off** and add a
  secondary promotional tag noting the specific expiry date for customer transparency.
- **Within 3 days of expiry date**: Remove from sales floor. Log remaining units in the
  waste management system. OTC medications past expiry must be disposed of through the
  licensed pharmaceutical waste vendor (contract ref: PWV-2024-0087).

### 1.3 Prescription (Rx) Pharmaceuticals — STRICT COMPLIANCE
Products classified under **Prescription Medications** are subject to **federal and state
regulatory compliance**. The following rules are absolute and non-negotiable:

- **Prescription medications must NEVER be discounted to the public.** Public-facing markdowns,
  promotions, BOGO offers, or any form of price reduction for Rx items are strictly prohibited
  for patient safety, regulatory compliance, and legal liability reasons.
- **Within 60 days of expiry date**: The batch must be flagged for a **"Vendor Return Credit"**
  under the standard 60-day commercial return window. The pharmacy manager must initiate a
  formal return authorization request (Form VRC-60) with the original pharmaceutical distributor.
- **Within 30 days of expiry date**: If a vendor return has not been processed, escalate to the
  district pharmacy supervisor. Document the delay in the compliance tracking system.
- **On expiry date**: Any remaining Rx inventory must be quarantined in the secure returns cage,
  logged in the DEA-compliant destruction manifest (if applicable for controlled substances),
  and processed through the licensed reverse distribution vendor.

## 2. Promotional Bundle Authorization Limits

### 2.1 Bundle Pricing Rules
- Promotional bundles may combine up to **4 near-expiry products** from the same department.
- The aggregate bundle discount must not exceed **60% off** the combined retail price.
- Bundle promotions require approval from the **department lead** (for bundles under $50 total
  retail value) or the **store manager** (for bundles $50 and above).
- All bundle promotions must be logged in the POS system under promotion code prefix **"NEB-"**
  (Near-Expiry Bundle) followed by the date and department code.

### 2.2 Cross-Department Bundles
- Cross-department bundles (e.g., combining dairy with bakery items) are permitted only when
  both departments have approved the markdown independently.
- Cross-department bundles must not include any pharmacy items (OTC or Rx).

## 3. Vendor Return Credit Procedures

### 3.1 Return Eligibility Window
- **Standard return window**: Products are eligible for vendor return credit if they are within
  **60 days of the expiry date** and the original distributor accepts returns for that SKU.
- **Extended return window**: Certain distributors offer a 90-day return window for specific
  product lines. Consult the vendor contract matrix (document: VCM-2024) before initiating.

### 3.2 Return Authorization Process
1. Generate a **Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA)** through the vendor portal or by
   contacting the distributor's returns department directly.
2. Pack items per the distributor's return shipping guidelines (typically original packaging,
   temperature-controlled shipping for cold-chain items).
3. Ship within **14 business days** of RMA issuance. Late shipments forfeit the credit.
4. Retain a copy of the shipping manifest and tracking number in the returns log.

### 3.3 Credit Application
- Vendor return credits are typically applied within **30–45 business days** after the
  distributor receives and inspects the returned goods.
- Credits appear as line-item adjustments on the next invoice cycle.
- Dispute any missing credits within **60 days** of the expected credit date.

## 4. Waste Reporting and Loss Prevention

### 4.1 Daily Waste Log
- Each department must submit a daily waste log by **end of business** documenting:
  - Product name, SKU, batch number, quantity disposed, reason code.
  - Reason codes: **EXP** (expired), **DMG** (damaged), **RCL** (recalled), **QA** (quality issue).

### 4.2 Shrinkage Thresholds
- Monthly waste exceeding **2% of departmental revenue** triggers an automatic review by the
  loss prevention team.
- Departments exceeding **3.5%** for two consecutive months require a corrective action plan
  submitted to the regional operations director.

### 4.3 Donation Program
- Non-expired but near-expiry food items (within 1 day of expiry) that cannot be sold may be
  donated to the store's partnered food bank (agreement ref: FB-LOCAL-2024).
- Donated items must be logged separately under reason code **DON** and are excluded from
  shrinkage calculations.

## 5. Compliance and Audit Requirements

### 5.1 Record Retention
- All markdown, bundle promotion, vendor return, and waste disposal records must be retained
  for a minimum of **7 years** per company policy and regulatory requirements.

### 5.2 Audit Frequency
- Internal audits of expiry management compliance are conducted **quarterly**.
- External regulatory audits (health department, pharmacy board) occur **annually** or as
  scheduled by the governing body.

### 5.3 Training
- All store associates handling perishable goods must complete the **"Expiry Management &
  Food Safety"** training module within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter.
- Pharmacy staff must additionally complete **"Rx Returns & Regulatory Compliance"** training,
  renewed annually with a passing score of 85% or higher.
